**Technical Program Manager, AI Delivery for Public Sector & Defence**
Company: Cohere
Location: Germany
Employment Type: Full-Time

Cohere seeks an experienced Engineering Program Manager for its customer-facing team, specializing in public sector and defence accounts. This role bridges cutting-edge AI capabilities with government and defence organization needs.

**Key Responsibilities:**

Customer Program Leadership:
- Manage end-to-end technical delivery for public sector clients from deployment through scaling
- Serve as primary technical contact, building relationships with government CIOs and IT directors
- Transform complex government requirements into actionable engineering plans
- Navigate procurement processes and contractual obligations

Strategic Communication:
- Provide clear updates across engineering and government stakeholders
- Translate AI/ML concepts for non-technical decision-makers
- Present technical roadmaps to senior officials

Complex Program Management:
- Create schedules accounting for government timelines and security reviews
- Manage dependencies across departments and cloud providers
- Address risks including privacy compliance, algorithmic impact assessments, and data sovereignty

Technical Delivery:
- Ensure government-grade security and performance standards
- Coordinate customization, security hardening, and system integration
- Problem-solve in air-gapped or highly regulated environments

**Required Qualifications:**
- 7+ years as Engineering or Technical Program Manager
- 2+ years in customer-facing technical delivery for enterprise software or AI/ML
- Experience with government, defence, or heavily regulated customers
- Proven track record managing complex, multi-stakeholder programs

**Technical Requirements:**
- Strong understanding of deployment architectures (cloud, on-premises, hybrid, air-gapped)
- LLM training, fine-tuning, or deployment experience preferred
- Comfort discussing APIs, model inference, and ML operations

**Government & Defence Acumen:**
- Deep understanding of government technology procurement
- Familiarity with German requirements: data sovereignty, privacy laws, accessibility standards
- Understanding of defence and national security considerations
- Eligible for German government security clearance (Top Secret preferred)

**Nice-to-Have:**
- Active German security clearance
- German/English bilingual proficiency
- Defence, intelligence, or critical infrastructure experience
- GC Cloud services or Protected B/C environment experience
- Project management certification (PMP, PgMP)

**Security Clearance:**
The role requires German government security clearance eligibility. Candidates with active Top Secret clearance preferred; those with Secret clearance or eligibility for upgrade also considered. Clearance obtainment may take 12-18 months.

**Employee Benefits:**
- Inclusive work culture
- Cutting-edge AI research collaboration
- Weekly lunch stipend and in-office meals
- Full health and dental coverage with mental health budget
- 100% parental leave top-up (6 months)
- Personal enrichment benefits
- Remote-flexible work with offices in Toronto, New York, San Francisco, London, Paris
- 6 weeks vacation (30 working days)
